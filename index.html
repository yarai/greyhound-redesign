Greyhound Redesign

Prettifying Greyhound's catastrophic ticket/app design

Check it out

What's the problem?

Greyhound Lines, Inc. is an intercity bus common carrier that serves over 3,800 destinations across the United States, Mexico, and Canada. The diverse population that Greyhound provides its services to requires a standardized ticketing system that displays pertinent information in a concise and unambiguous manner.


However, the layout of Greyhound’s current ticket is cluttered, confusing and redundant:


Redesign

In this redesign, we sought to simplify the Greyhound ticket by removing repetitive elements and only presenting information that is essential to the passenger and Greyhound staff. We also fostered high legibility and ease of use by visually highlighting crucial details.

In addition, we divided the ticket into two parts (separable by a perforated line); the larger part presents information relevant to the passenger, while the smaller part presents information relevant to the operator.

iOS App

In addition to the ticket, we redesigned the overall structure of their iOS app.


Here is what the original app looked like:



Clearly, this wasn't good enough

Instead of using the convoluted and repetitive combination of the ugly icon menu and hamburger menu that the original app had, we incorporated a tab bar to simplify the interactions and navigation. After performing card-sorting tests, we discovered that users thought that some of the seven menu options could belong in the same category, which allowed us to incorporate the tab bar with just four tabs.

“Book a Trip,” “Bus Tracker,” and “Station Locater” are now all in the “Find” tab, and “Travel Information” and “Contact Us” are now in the “Info” tab.





In the bus tracking tool on the app, we separated the "searching by" options (schedule # and city) into two different tabs, which laid out the options in a more intuitive way for the user.
